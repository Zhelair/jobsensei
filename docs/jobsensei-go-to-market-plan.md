# JobSensei Go-To-Market Plan

Last updated: 2026-05-22

## Goal

Make JobSensei easier to sell by leading with one urgent outcome instead of a long feature list.

Primary selling promise:

`Paste the job description. Practice the interview before it happens.`

Secondary proof:
- Gap analysis
- STAR answer building
- Learning and quizzes
- Job tracker and prep notes
- Privacy-first browser experience

The goal is not to market JobSensei as "15 AI tools in one app."
The goal is to market it as a serious interview-prep system for people who actually have interviews coming up.

## Best Audience To Start With

Start narrow.

Primary audience:
- Mid-level to senior job seekers
- People with an interview scheduled in the next 7-14 days
- Professionals who want structured prep, not generic AI fluff

Best starting niche:
- Fintech
- Compliance
- AML / FRAML
- Risk
- Operations

Why this niche first:
- Founder credibility is strongest here
- Messaging can be more specific
- Specific audiences convert better than broad "everyone job hunting" audiences

Secondary audience later:
- Tech professionals
- Marketers
- Career changers
- Sales and customer success professionals

## Positioning

### Recommended one-liners

Use these repeatedly and do not rotate too much at the start.

Option 1:
`JobSensei helps serious job seekers practice real interviews based on the exact job description.`

Option 2:
`AI interview prep gym for people who do not want to wing the interview.`

Option 3:
`Paste the JD, find your gaps, and practice the interview before the real one.`

### Recommended short pitch

`JobSensei is a browser-based interview prep app for serious job seekers. Paste the job description, get likely questions, practice in mock interviews, spot your gaps, and tighten your answers before the real interview.`

### What to lead with

- Interview Simulator
- Drill Mode
- JD-based question practice
- Gap Analysis

### What to mention later as proof or depth

- Learning section
- Quizzes
- STAR Builder
- Job tracker
- Notes and exports
- Privacy-first setup

### What not to lead with

- Too many tools at once
- "AI-powered" as the main headline
- Privacy as the main hook
- Every feature in a single post

Privacy matters, but it should support trust, not carry the whole sale.

## Offer Strategy

Do not start with a broad free-for-all.

Start with a tight beta offer:

`I am giving free access to 15 people who have a real interview coming up in the next 10 days. In return, I want blunt feedback on what helped and what felt weak.`

Why this is better:
- Attracts real users instead of freebie collectors
- Gives you better product feedback
- Creates urgency without fake scarcity
- Gives you a clean CTA for posts and comments

Optional follow-up offer:

`If you want zero setup, supporter access unlocks the AI side without needing your own API key.`

That should come after interest, not before it.

## Best Channels Right Now

### 1. LinkedIn

Best use:
- Founder story
- Practical job-search lessons
- Product walkthrough clips

Why:
- Audience is already career-focused
- Easier to build trust over time
- Native video and text both work

### 2. Reddit

Best use:
- Useful posts
- Honest founder story
- Feedback requests in communities that allow them

Why:
- Strong upside if the post feels human and helpful
- Strong downside if it feels promotional

Rule of thumb:
- Teach first
- Link lightly
- Respect each subreddit's rules

### 3. TikTok / YouTube Shorts

Best use:
- Screen recordings
- Before/after flows
- One feature per video

Why:
- JobSensei demos well visually
- Interview Simulator is easy to understand when seen

### 4. Facebook Groups

Best use:
- Only selective testing
- Communities that clearly allow helpful tool shares or beta invites

Why not first:
- Group quality varies a lot
- Promotion tolerance is inconsistent
- Harder to build repeatable momentum

## Fourteen-Day Plan

The objective is not vanity reach.
The objective is to learn which message gets people to ask for access or try the app.

### Week 1

Day 1:
- Publish one founder-style LinkedIn post
- Publish one short demo video

Day 2:
- Reply to every comment
- DM only people who explicitly ask for access

Day 3:
- Publish one useful Reddit post with no hard sell

Day 4:
- Publish one short video about Drill Mode or Gap Analysis

Day 5:
- Publish one practical LinkedIn post with interview advice

Day 6:
- Collect feedback from any new testers

Day 7:
- Review what got saves, comments, DMs, or access requests

### Week 2

Day 8:
- Publish a founder update: what people responded to

Day 9:
- Publish one Reddit feedback-request post in an allowed community

Day 10:
- Publish one short walkthrough of a full JD-to-practice flow

Day 11:
- Share one specific lesson from a tester or your own use

Day 12:
- Repost the strongest angle in a fresh format

Day 13:
- Write down the best hooks and objections

Day 14:
- Decide which positioning angle won

## What To Measure

Track only a few things at first:
- Access requests
- Actual activated users
- Which feature made them care
- Which post angle got the best response
- Which objections repeated

Do not obsess over impressions yet.

The most useful questions:
- Did this post make anyone ask for access?
- Did they care about interviews, gaps, privacy, or "all-in-one" convenience?
- Did the person sound urgent or just curious?

## Reddit Posts

### Reddit Post 1: Useful and low-promo

Title:
`One thing that made interview prep more useful for me`

Body:

`The biggest change for me was stopping "silent prep."`

`Reading answers and thinking "yeah, I would say something like that" is not the same as actually saying it out loud under pressure.`

`What helped was taking a real job description, predicting the likely questions, and then practicing follow-up questions until the weak parts showed up.`

`That exposed stuff I would have missed otherwise: vague examples, weak results, and answers that sounded better in my head than out loud.`

`I ended up building a small tool for this workflow because I wanted the practice to be based on the exact JD, not generic interview lists.`

`Curious how other people here prepare for follow-up questions specifically.`

### Reddit Post 2: Feedback request

Title:
`Built a JD-based interview prep app and looking for blunt feedback from real job seekers`

Body:

`I built a browser-based interview prep app called JobSensei because I was frustrated with generic prep tools.`

`The part I cared about most was using the actual job description to generate likely questions, practice mock interviews, and see where my answers were weak.`

`It also does gap analysis and some supporting prep stuff, but the main point is helping you walk into the interview sharper.`

`I am looking for a small group of people who are actively interviewing and willing to give blunt feedback.`

`If your community allows it, I can share access with a few people here. If not, I am still happy to hear what would make a tool like this genuinely useful instead of gimmicky.`

## Short Video Scripts

Keep these simple. Screen recording plus calm voiceover is enough.

### Video 1: Paste JD -> practice interview

Hook:
`If you have an interview coming up, do not just read sample answers.`

Script:
`This is JobSensei. I paste the job description here, it pulls out the likely interview themes, then I jump straight into a mock interview based on that exact role.`

`The useful part is not just the questions. It is the pushback. If my answer is vague, the app keeps pressing until I get specific.`

`That is much closer to what a real interview feels like.`

Ending:
`If you are interviewing soon, I am giving a few people free access for feedback.`

### Video 2: Drill Mode

Hook:
`Most interview tools are too nice.`

Script:
`This mode is called Drill Mode. The point is simple: if your answer is weak, it should feel weak now, not during the real interview.`

`I wanted something that would challenge vague answers, push for examples, and make me tighten the story before the stakes were real.`

Ending:
`It is uncomfortable, but that is exactly why it helps.`

### Video 3: Gap Analysis

Hook:
`Before you apply, check whether the job is actually a fit.`

Script:
`I paste the job description and my background, and JobSensei gives me a rough fit view, likely strengths, likely gaps, and things I need to tighten before applying or interviewing.`

`That is useful because not every application deserves the same amount of time.`

Ending:
`It helps you prepare smarter, not just harder.`

## Founder Tone Rules

Stay close to this tone:
- Honest
- Specific
- Useful
- Calm

Avoid:
- Big startup language
- "Revolutionizing job search"
- Fake urgency
- Overclaiming traction
- Listing every feature in every post

## What To Say If Someone Asks "Why not just use ChatGPT?"

Suggested answer:

`You can use ChatGPT for pieces of this, and that is completely fair.`

`What I wanted was a workflow built for job seekers instead of a blank chat box: JD-based mock interviews, follow-up pressure, gap analysis, STAR prep, and one place to keep the prep together.`

`So the value is less "AI exists" and more "the process is already structured for this exact problem."`

## Key Reminder

At the start, do not try to prove that JobSensei does everything.

Try to prove one thing:

`It helps real job seekers prepare better for real interviews.`

If people believe that first, the rest of the product becomes easier to sell.
