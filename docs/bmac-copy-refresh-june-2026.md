# JobSensei Copy Refresh

Last updated: June 19, 2026

## Core call

JobSensei is the product.

The Chrome extension should be a sharp acquisition wedge, not the whole sales pitch.

Best framing:

`Capture a live job post into your JobSensei workspace, then continue prep there.`

Mention free access early enough to remove friction, but do not make the extension sound like a pricing page.

Best sequence:

1. Capture the page.
2. Send it into JobSensei.
3. Start free.
4. Upgrade later only if needed.

## Current credits model to describe

Current in-app values from `src/lib/credits.js`:

- Hosted AI cost: `31 credits` per request
- Free allowance: `531 credits` every `31 days`
- Pro allowance: `53,000 credits` every `31 days`

Plain-English explanation:

- `Free` gives about `17 hosted AI requests` every 31 days.
- `Pro` gives about `1,709 hosted AI requests` every 31 days.
- `BYOK` means users can connect their own API key and not use hosted JobSensei credits.

Recommended simple wording:

`Free is enough to properly prep for at least one real job opportunity each month.`

## Recommendation for the extension/store angle

Do this:

- Lead with capture.
- Mention that it works with the JobSensei app.
- Mention `start free` as a trust reducer.
- Keep the credits explanation short inside extension/store copy.

Do not do this:

- Sell the extension like it is the full JobSensei experience by itself.
- Lead with pricing, Pro, or Buy Me a Coffee inside the extension.
- Overload the popup with plan math.

Recommended one-liner:

`JobSensei Capture sends a live job post into your JobSensei workspace so you can track it, research it, and prepare around the exact JD.`

Recommended short access line:

`Start free in JobSensei. Monthly AI credits are included, and BYOK or Pro stay optional.`

## Reddit angle

Best approach:

- Do not sell the credits first.
- Lead with the real pain: people never practice against the exact JD.
- Mention free only after the workflow is clear.

Body:

`I was frustrated by how much job-search advice stops at CV edits or generic interview tips.`

## Tone reminder

The winning tone here is:

- `serious prep`
- `exact JD`
- `start free`
- `upgrade only if needed`

The weaker tone is:

- `all-in-one AI supertool`
- `buy this extension`
- `huge feature dump`
