## 📌 POST 3 — Gap Analysis / Red Flags

**Title:** Stop Applying Blindly 🛑 Know Your Score First

**Body:**

Here's a habit that will save you hours of wasted applications:

Before you hit "Apply" → run the job description through Gap Analysis.

JobSensei's Gap Analysis tool does three things:

**1️⃣ Gap Analysis**
Paste the JD + your background. Get:
- Fit score (0–100%)
- Your top strengths for this role
- Your key gaps (with an action plan)
- Whether you should actually apply

**2️⃣ Application Scoring**
Beyond fit — it scores your *application* specifically. Great Fit? Stretch? Long Shot? You'll know before you invest the time.

**3️⃣ Red Flag Detector 🚩**
This one's underrated. It reads the JD for:
- Toxic culture signals ("we work hard and play hard" 🙄)
- Unrealistic expectations ("5+ years in a 3-year-old framework")
- Vague compensation ("competitive salary")
- Signs of high turnover or poor management

Know what you're walking into *before* the interview.

Everything saves to your project, so you build a history of analyses and can compare roles side-by-side.

## 📌 POST 8 — Quick Tips Style (great for carousels / captions)

**Title:** 5 Things Most People Get Wrong in Job Interviews 🎤

**Body:**

Practiced this stuff with JobSensei's AI and realized how common these mistakes are:

**1. Answering what was asked, not what was meant**
When they ask "tell me about yourself," they don't want your life story. They want: who you are professionally + why you're here + why you're good at this. Practice that.

**2. Vague "team player" answers**
Generic adjectives don't land. Specific stories do. "I collaborated well" = forgettable. "I caught a £200K fraud pattern my team missed by building a quick analysis in Python" = memorable. Build your Story Bank.

**3. Never researching the red flags in the JD**
That "fast-paced environment with a wear-many-hats approach" might mean underfunded and chaotic. Read the JD critically. Run it through a Red Flag Detector if you can.

**4. Skipping salary practice**
Most people don't counter because they're scared. Counter with confidence because you practiced. Even once. Even with an AI.

**5. Not tracking their applications**
You applied to 30 roles and forgot half of them. You missed a follow-up. You showed up to the interview not remembering your own notes. A Kanban board fixes this in 10 minutes.
